The Greatest Meeting

Author:
Majid Amini

The astonishing life stories of two giant emancipators of the human race, Rumi and Shams-e Tabrizi, and their legendary meeting that took place on November 29, 1244, which inspired Rumi to become the most prolific (and revered) mystic poet of all time. Shams, a rebel with the noble cause of elevating human stature to the highest-imaginable level, seeks to enable individuals to become the determiners of their own values and destinies by redefining religion and thereby making it relevant to our earthly existence.

Escape From Paradise

Author:
Majid Amini

The lives of a group of Iranians and a young American woman with her two children are seriously threatened in the devastating aftermath of the bloody Iran revolution of 1979. Combined, their lives make a tapestry that reflects the entire colorful canvas of society’s different political persuasions and social classes. For reasons of their own, each is blacklisted and cannot leave the country through the normal means. Unbeknownst to each other, they individually employ the services of an underground smuggling ring, run by a profiteer of the revolution, to guide them across the Iran-Turkish border.

Dreams of a Native Son

Dreams of a Native Son. He comes to the United States for an education, planning to return to his native land to use his hard won knowledge for the benefit of his people. Lost in this society after the completion of his studies, he is tortured and torn apart by his appetite for the good life which he finds so illusive. Floundering, he tries to hide his profound misery in physical relationships with lonely women. A series of dramatic and tragic events along with the influence of an old Indian woman, an African friend and an aged famous author alters his path of self destruction. He must face the agonizing decision to leave the shattered pieces of his life and his missing son behind or continue his homeless existence in the vain hope of finding his roots.

The Howling Leopard

The Howling Leopard is an enchanting tale of a seven year old boy, a german dog and a mosnsrously huge servant. The servant, a man with a flamboyant imagination, weaves fantastic war stroies full of heroic and daring deeds in which he always plays a leading role. The young boy is captivated by the stories and the romantic figure the servant portrays, he worships and idolizes the man only to later find out he has been deceived. Upon this realization, a 'war' breaks out between the two and is maintained throughout the remainder of their relationship. It is a touching tale of love and betrayal, full of daring exploits, childhood pranks and the sweet and bitter pain of growing up.

The Sunset Drifters

The Sunset Drifters is a delightful voyage of self-discovery and transformation of four young foreign students Marxists. Set in California during the 1960s, it narrates the hilarious saga of "the four musketeers", Cock-Eyed Jack, Cool-Hand Jeff, Fred the Magnificent and their sole follower, tirelessly struggle to employ the disenchanted minorities, Blacks, American-Indians, Mexican-Americans, for the ultimate Proletariat Revolution in America.
